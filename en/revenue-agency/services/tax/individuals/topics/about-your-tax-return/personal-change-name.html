Change your name

You may be looking for:

  • Change a business operating name
 

If you change your name, tell the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) as soon as possible. How you tell us about your new name depends on how many parts of your name you are changing.

We do not accept name changes online.

Change either your first name or last name

By phone

Processing time: Immediate

You can change your name by phone if you haven't already changed one of your names. For example, if you previously changed your first name, you cannot change your last name by phone.

Exception: Changing back to your birth name

If you’re changing back to your birth name, and we have that name on file, you can do it by phone (documentation not required).

Before you call
Have all of the following information ready to verify your identity:
  • Social insurance number (SIN)
  • Full name and date of birth
  • Complete address
  • Assessed tax return, notice of assessment or reassessment, or other tax document

If you have a CRA account, you can also sign into your account to access your tax information.

Make a change for someone else

You can only call on behalf of someone else in the following 2 situations:

  • The person is present on the call to verify their identity in English or French and provides verbal authorization for you to speak for them

  • You are the person's legal representative with full legal authority to act as them

    For details: Authorize a representative

By mail or fax

Processing time: 4 to 6 weeks after we receive your documents

You must provide documentation if you have already changed your name.

Send or fax a letter to your tax centre with the following information:

  • Original or certified true copy of one of the following documents:
    • A name change certificate from a provincial/territorial vital statistics department
    • A court order issued under an act on change of name
  • Your old and new names
  • Your social insurance number
  • Your signature

Change both your first name and last name

Changes to both a first and last name must be done by mail or fax.

By mail or fax:

Processing time: 4 to 6 weeks after we receive your documents

Send a letter to your tax centre with the following information:

  • Original or certified true copy of one of the following documents:
    • A name change certificate from a provincial/territorial vital statistics department
    • A court order issued under an act on change of name
  • Your old and new names
  • Your social insurance number
  • Your signature

Change your name with other government departments

The CRA will not share your name change with other government departments. You must do this separately. The only exception to this is if you allowed CRA to share this information with Elections Canada on your tax return.
