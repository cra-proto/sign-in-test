CRA account help

Representatives: Request authorization

You may be looking for:

  • Represent someone who died
 

After you have registered for Represent a Client (RAC), you must request and receive authorization to access a client's account.

Once you are authorized, you can access and manage information on behalf of individuals, businesses, or trusts.

Get authorized to access an account

You can initiate the authorization process in the following ways:

Use your Represent a Client identifier

Your client, friend, or family member can authorize you with your RAC identifier.

  1. Give your RAC identifier to your client

    You received a representative identifier (RepID) when you registered with Represent a Client. You might also have registered a business number (BN) or created a group identifier (GroupID). You can give your RepID, BN, or GroupID to individuals, businesses, or trusts so they can authorize you.

    Learn more:

    • Set up your business in Represent a Client
    • Set up a GroupID in Represent a Client
     

    RAC identifier

    Any of the following can be used as your Represent a Client (RAC) identifier:

    • RepID
    • GroupID
    • Business number (BN), when you have registered it with RAC

  2. Your client authorizes you

    Your client, friend, or family member must access their CRA account and enter your RepID, BN or GroupID to add you as an authorized representative.

    Once they have signed in to their account and confirmed your authorization with the RAC identifier you gave them, you will have access to their information through your RAC account.

Request authorization through Represent a Client

If you have your client's information, you can initiate a request for authorization through your RAC account.

  1. Submit your request through Represent a Client

    1. Sign in to your RAC account
    2. Select Authorization request
    3. Choose the appropriate RAC identifier (if prompted) and the type of client and select Submit
    4. Select Add authorization
    5. Provide some identifying information about your client
    6. Choose the confirmation method:

      • Your client will confirm in their CRA account

      • You will provide your client's tax information from a recent notice of assessment that was processed at least 6 months ago

    7. Add the level of authorization, expiry date, and the program accounts you are requesting access to (if necessary), then Confirm

  2. Wait for your client to confirm (if you chose this method)

    Most clients will need to sign in to their account and confirm your request within 10 business days.

    If your client is a primary trustee, they must sign into their RAC account to access their Trust account and confirm your request there.

    Learn more: Confirm my representative's authorization request

Get authorized for offline access only

Your client can fill out Form AUT-01, Authorize a Representative for Offline Access. This form will give you access to their tax information over the phone, by mail, by fax, or in person. This form does not provide online access.

Send the form to the appropriate CRA tax centre listed on the form within 6 months of the date it is signed.
