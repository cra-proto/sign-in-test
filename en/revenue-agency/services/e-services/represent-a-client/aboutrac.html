About Represent a Client

Represent a Client (RAC) gives authorized representatives secure, quick, and easy online access to tax-related information for the individuals, businesses and trusts they represent. Transactions in RAC are processed immediately, and information is up-to-the-minute.

What's new and updated

Get quick access by validating your identity when you register

Interac® document verification service (DVS) is an alternative identity validation option that allows Canadians to register for and access the CRA accounts in real-time. DVS provides the option for digital identity validation through document verification when registering for the CRA’s accounts. Canadians choosing this option will no longer need to wait up to 10 business days to receive a CRA security code by paper mail in order to register and access the CRA accounts. The process requires a mobile device to take a picture of the user’s government-issued photo identification and of themselves to verify their identity. To use this service you must:

  • Be 16 years of age or older
  • Have an accepted government-issued photo identification
  • Have a mobile device with a working camera

The following original government-issued documents are accepted:

  • Canadian passport
  • Canadian driver's license
  • Provincial or territorial photo ID card

My Trust Account service updates

New services and improvements have been added to My Trust Account, giving users more options to manage their trust account online. New features include: view returns, mail, communication center, account notifications, account balance information, and online payment options.

Email notification updates

Users of the CRA accounts will receive an email notification:

  • Letting them know they have successfully enrolled in Multi-factor authentication with either the telephone option, passcode grid option, or authenticator application option
  • When they have been permanently or temporarily locked out of their account.

Authorization request signature page removed in Represent a Client

When a representative submits an authorization request to represent a business client in Represent a Client, the authorization signature page will no longer be generated as the authorization process will be finalized in My Business Account. If an authorization request was submitted in Represent a Client before May 15, 2023, the generated signature page will still be required.

If the business does not have access to use the CRA’s digital services, see Exclusions to the Confirm my Representative service for more information.

Who can use Represent a Client

A representative can be anyone who would like to access information and services on behalf of individuals and businesses. Examples include, but are not limited to:

  • An individual who is involved in the tax affairs of individuals and/or businesses, including:
    • A friend or family member
    • An executor, power of attorney or legal guardian
    • An employee who does payroll/GST/corporate etc. for their employer
    • A bookkeeper
    • A public trustee
    • A lawyer
    • A financial planner
  • An owner or employee of a business that provides tax or payroll services for clients
  • A non-resident representative living in the United States

Responsibilities of authorized representatives

A client is anyone who authorizes you to be their representative, including:

  • A family member or friend
  • A business
  • Your employer

RAC identifiers

There are 3 different RAC identifiers you can use to gain authorization and access to someone else's information:

RepID

Everyone who registers with RAC will receive a Rep ID.

Share this with your clients, family or friends to authorize you as their representative. You can also give your RepID to your employer to gain access to their clients' information.

If you lose your RepID

If you lose or forget your RepID, you do not need to register for another one. To view your RepID:

  1. Sign in to Represent a Client
  2. Select Review and update
  3. Select View and update my registration information
Business number (BN)

Register your business with RAC to manage the access of employees to your client's information. Clients can authorize a business as an authorized representative, and only the employees associated to the business can access client information.

For help with setting up your business to use RAC and associating employees to your business, go to: Set up your business in Represent a Client

GroupID

Any representative with a RAC account can create a group that will be identified by a unique GroupID. Any representative associated to the GroupID will have online access to the tax information of taxpayers that have authorized the GroupID.

For more information, go to: Set up a GroupID in Represent a Client

Features available in Represent a Client

The features in RAC that are available to a representative are different for personal tax accounts, business tax accounts, trust tax accounts, and non-resident tax accounts.

You can access client accounts from the RAC main menu, by entering your client's SIN, business number or trust number in the appropriate field.
