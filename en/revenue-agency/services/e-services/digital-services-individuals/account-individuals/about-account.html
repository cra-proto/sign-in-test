About My Account

My Account is a secure portal that lets you view your personal income tax and benefit information and manage your tax affairs online. Transactions in My Account are processed immediately, and information is up-to-the-minute.

What's new and updated

Interac® document verification service (DVS)

Document verification service (DVS) is an alternative identity validation option that allows Canadians to register for and access the CRA accounts in real-time. DVS provides the option for digital identity validation through document verification when registering for the CRA’s accounts. Canadians choosing this option will no longer need to wait up to 10 business days to receive a CRA security code by paper mail in order to register and access the CRA accounts. The process requires a mobile device to take a picture of the user’s government-issued photo identification and of themselves to verify their identity. The following are the requirements for this service:

  • Be 16 years of age or older
  • Have an accepted government-issued document
  • Have a mobile device with a working camera

The following original government-issued documents are accepted:

  • Canadian passport
  • Canadian driver's license
  • Provincial/Territorial photo ID card

Language of correspondence

A new service called "Language of correspondence" was added to the Profile page in My Account allowing users to update their preferred language of correspondence between English and French.

Who can use My Account

Anyone who can register for a CRA account can use My Account.

Signing in on behalf of someone else

If you want to sign in to My Account on behalf of someone else (including friends and family members), you must use Represent a Client to access their information.

My Account of a deceased person

You cannot use the CRA accounts to access information for deceased persons, unless you are that person's representative. If not, you will have to contact us.

Representatives (for example, an executor) can access My Account on behalf of a deceased person using Represent a Client.

Sign-in options

There are 3 options for signing in:

CRA user ID and password
This option lets you create a unique CRA user ID and password to sign in to your account.
Learn more: About CRA user ID and password
Interac® sign-in partner
This option lets you sign in with your online banking information.
Learn more: About Interac sign-in partner
Provincial digital ID
This option lets you sign in with BC or Alberta ID information you may already have.
Learn more: About provincial sign-in partner

Features in My Account

After signing in to My Account, you can:

  • Change your address or phone number or other personal information
  • Check your benefit and credit payments
  • Get email notifications
  • Set up direct deposit
  • Track your refund
  • Use Auto-fill My Return when you file online using certified software
  • View or change your return
  • View your RRSP limit
  • Set up a payment plan

Until you receive your security code and complete your registration, you will only have access to limited features in your CRA Account.

Account availability

Access your account during our available hours of service. If you are having difficulty, you might need to update your browser.

Hours of service

The CRA accounts are available 21 hours a day. Due to daily maintenance activities, the accounts are not available from 3 am to 6 am (Eastern time).

Hours of operation
  • Newfoundland Standard Time: 7:30 am to 4:30 am
  • Atlantic Standard Time: 7 am to 4 am
  • Eastern Standard Time: 6 am to 3 am
  • Central Standard Time: 5 am to 2 am
  • Mountain Standard Time: 4 am to 1 am
  • Pacific Standard Time: 3 am to 12 am (midnight)

Service maintenance

The CRA accounts may also be unavailable during the following maintenance schedule:

  • Friday, February 2, 2024 to Monday, February 5, 2024
  • Friday, May 10, 2024 to Monday, May 13, 2024
  • Friday, October 18, 2024 to Monday, October 21, 2024

If you require any of these services during these times, please contact us by phone.

Browser requirements

If you are not able to access your account, you might need to upgrade your browser version to meet the security standards. To ensure that you have uninterrupted access to the CRA accounts, your browser must:

  • Support TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Have TLS 1.2 or higher enabled
  • Have Cookies enabled
  • Have JavaScript enabled
Cookies

These services use persistent cookies. These persistent cookies will not store any identifying data.

Troubleshooting tips to access your account

Here are some tips if you experience issues accessing the CRA accounts:

  • Refresh your browser
  • Try using a different browser
  • Try using a different device
  • Clear your cache, delete your cookies and internet history
  • Try using the data on your cellphone as opposed to WIFI
  • Restart your computer
