Register for My Account for individuals

Follow the steps below to register for access to your CRA account.

  1. Decide if you need to register

    If you have already registered for a CRA account using a CRA user ID and password or an Interac sign-in partner, you cannot register again. You must use the same sign-in information to access the following accounts:

    • My Account
    • My Business Account
    • Represent a Client

    If you already have an account

  2. Gather your documents

    When you register with a CRA user ID and password or an Interac sign-in partner, you will need all of the following:

    • Your Social Insurance Number (SIN)
      • Alternatives: a Temporary Tax Number (TTN) or Individual Tax Number (ITN)
    • Your date of birth
    • Your most recent tax return from the current or previous tax year
      Find out which tax information we will ask for

      You must enter the amount you reported on:

      • Line 15000
      • Another line number

      Use the amounts you entered on the most recent tax return you filed. Your tax return must be assessed and from either the current or previous tax year.

      Use only the amounts that you reported on your return, even if your return was reassessed and the amounts have changed.

    For faster access to all features, you will also need a mobile device with a working camera and one of the following:

    • Your Canadian passport
    • Your Canadian driver's license
    • Your provincial photo ID card
    Have your documents ready before you begin

    During registration, your session will expire after 18 minutes of inactivity. Gather your documents before you begin to avoid your session timing out.

  3. Choose how to access your account

    Choose which option to register with. You will use the same option each time you sign in.

    Find the best option for your situation

    You can choose one of 3 ways to sign into your account.

    CRA user ID and password

    Choose CRA user ID and password if you want to create credentials that you only use with your CRA sign in accounts.

    Interac sign-in partner

    Using an Interac sign-in partner is a good option if you are registered for an online account with one of our sign-in partner banks or credit unions and want to use the same sign-in information.

    Provincial sign-in partner

    If you already have a BC Services Card or an Alberta.ca Account, you can use them to immediately access your My Account.

    The first time you sign in, you will need to enter your SIN to verify your identity. You will not need to use Interac document verification service or a CRA security code to verify your identity.

    You can register with your provincial sign-in partner even if you have not filed your taxes in the last 2 years.

    You cannot use your provincial sign-in partner to access My Business Account, Represent a Client, and certain services like Express Notice of Assessment, or Auto-fill My Return. If you would like to use those accounts and services, you can still register for another sign-in option.

    GC Key

    You cannot use GC Key to sign into My Account.

    • Create a CRA user ID and password
      Help with CRA User ID and password

      Choose memorable security questions

      You will be asked to choose 5 security questions that will be used verify your identity in different situations, for example, when signing in, if you forget your user ID, or if you sign in from a new device.

      Your security questions and answers should be easy for you to remember but hard for others to guess. Answers that can change over time or have more than one answer can be hard to remember.

      More help with CRA user ID and password

    • Register with an Interac® sign-in partner
      Help with Interac sign-in partners

      Do not use auto-complete on a shared device

      Do not use your browser's auto-complete for your online banking credentials if you register on a shared device.

      If you sign in with someone else's banking credentials by mistake and link your SIN to them, the other person will have access to your CRA account.

      Your banking and tax information is never shared

      • Your banking information and the identity of your financial institution are not shared with the Government of Canada.

      • None of your personal tax information will be shared with your financial institution.

      More help with Interac sign-in partners

    • Register with a provincial sign-in partner (BC, Alberta)
      Help with provincial sign-in partners

      To register this way, you must have an account with one of our supported provincial partners:

      • BC Services Card
      • Alberta.ca Account

      The first time you sign in, you will need to enter your SIN to verify your identity. You will not need to use Interac document verification service or a CRA security code to verify your identity.

      You can register with your provincial sign-in partner even if you have not filed your taxes in the last 2 years.

      You cannot use your provincial sign-in partner to access My Business Account, Represent a Client, and certain services like Express Notice of Assessment, or Auto-fill My Return. If you would like to use those accounts and services, you can still register for another sign-in option.

      More help with provincial sign-in partners

  4. Verify your identity

    Choose an option to verify your identity:

    • Submit a photo of yourself and your ID document Option 1 of 2

      You can use the Interac document verification service to verify your identity immediately.

      Use a mobile device to take a real-time picture of yourself and an accepted identification document.

      You must be 16 years of age or older to use this service.

    • Wait for your CRA security code Option 2 of 2

      If you can't use the document verification service, a CRA security code will be sent to you by mail within 5 to 10 days.

      Will user be asked to enter their postal code or just verify if it matches what is on file? What happens if the wrong postal code is on file? Do they need to call or update their address before they can register?

      We will send your CRA security code to the address we have on file. Make sure your address is up to date.

      You will need to sign into your account and enter your CRA security code before it expires.

    Get limited access before verifying your identity

    You do not need to verify your identity to access the following features:

    • View status of your tax return
    • View RRSP deduction limit and TFSA contribution room
    • View notice of assessment or reassessment
    • Request a remittance voucher
    • Pay by pre-authorized debit (create new agreement)
    • Change notification preferences

    After you verify your identity, you will have access to additional features such as applying for benefits and updating your personal information.

  5. Enroll in multi-factor authentication (MFA)

    Most users will be required to enter a one-time passcode in addition to their password.

    Choose an option to get your one-time passcode:

    • By phone Option 1 of 2

      You will need to provide at least one cell or landline number. You will be sent a one-time passcode by phone. You must enter this number to sign into your account.

    • With passcode grid Option 2 of 3

      The system will generate a unique passcode grid that you will need to save or print. You will use this grid every time you sign into your account.

      Your passcode grid will expire after 18 months. Make sure you sign in and generate a new one before the expiry date.

    • With an authenticator app Option 3 of 3

      You can use a third-party authenticator app to generate your time-based one-time passcode.

      When prompted, use the app on your mobile device to either scan a QR code or manually enter the setup key. The app can then be used to generate your one-time passcode.

    Avoid getting locked out of your account

    Enrolling in multiple MFA options will help ensure that you can still access your account if you change your phone number, misplace your passcode grid, or delete the authenticator app. When you sign into your account, you can update your current MFA settings or enroll in additional MFA options.

    If your account becomes locked due to entering the wrong one-time passcode too many times, contact us by phone to have your account unlocked.

    More help with multi-factor authentication

  6. Complete your identity verification

    Complete the verification option that you chose in step 4:

    • Interac document verification service Option 1 of 2

      Does this step still happen at the end of registration???

      Follow the instructions to complete this process on your mobile device.

      Avoid losing your progress

      During registration, your session will expire after 18 minutes of inactivity. Complete your document verification within that time to avoid your session timing out.

    • CRA security code Option 2 of 2

      After you receive your CRA security code in the mail, sign into My Account and enter your code before it expires.

      Get a new CRA security code

      If your code expires, gets lost, or has not arrived after 14 days, you can contact us by phone to get a new one.

Help with your account

Common issues for registration

You have not filed your taxes in the past 2 years

If you have not filed your taxes in the past 2 years, you will not be able to register using a CRA user ID and password or an Interac sign-in partner.

For information on how to file, go to Get ready to do your taxes.

If you live in BC or Alberta, you may be able to use a provincial sign-in partner to sign in.

You have already registered for an account

Use your existing credentials

If you have already registered for an account, Sign in with your existing account credentials.

If you are signing into My Business Account for the first time, you will be asked for your 9-digit business number.

If you have forgotten your sign-in information, go to: Your user ID and password.

Change your sign-in option

If you registered with a Provincial Partner, you can also register with a CRA user ID and password or Interac sign-in partner.

If you registered with a CRA user ID and password or Interac sign-in partner, you can:

  • Sign into your current Interac sign-in partner and switch to a different bank or credit union
  • Sign in with your CRA user ID and password and revoke your credential so you can register again
  • Contact us by phone to revoke your credential so you can register again

Find out more about Changing your sign in information.

Your banking and tax information is never shared

  • Your banking information and the identity of your financial institution are not shared with the Government of Canada.

  • None of your personal tax information will be shared with your financial institution.

Choose memorable security questions

You will be asked to choose 5 security questions that will be used to recover your account if you forget your password.

It is a good idea to choose questions that have factual answers. Answers that can change over time or have more than one answer can be hard to remember.

For example, it may be easier to remember the make of your first car. It may be harder to remember the name of a childhood friend that you might sometimes write in full or sometimes as a nickname.

Shared devices: Do not use auto-complete for banking credentials

Do not use your browser's auto-complete for your online banking credentials if you register on a shared device.

If you sign in with someone else's banking credentials by mistake and link your SIN to them, the other person will have access to your CRA account.

Accepted identification documents

We accept the following original government-issued documents:

  • Your Canadian passport
  • Your Canadian driver's license
  • Your provincial photo ID card

We do not accept:

  • Your provincial health card
  • Documents that you have reported as lost or stolen
  • Expired or damaged documents
  • Photocopies

Tax returns

You will be asked to enter the amount you reported on:

  • Line 15000
  • Another variable line number

Use the numbers that you reported on the most recent tax return that you filed. Your tax return must be assessed and from either the current or previous tax year.

Use only the numbers that you reported on your return, even if your return was reassessed and the numbers have changed.

One-time passcode by phone

When you sign in, you can choose to receive your one-time passcode by:

  • Text message
  • Automated phone call from a toll-free number

One-time passcode by passcode grid

The passcode grid is a table made up of numbered rows and lettered columns, similar to a Bingo card.

We will ask for a specific combination of rows and columns to create your one-time passcode.
