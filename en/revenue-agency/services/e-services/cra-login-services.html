Common issues

You forgot your CRA user ID or password

More help with CRA user ID and password

You did not receive your one-time passcode

If you have a new phone number

If you can no longer access your old phone number, and you have not already added your new phone number to your multi-factor authentication (MFA) options, you will have to contact us by phone.

Authenticator app and passcode grid

If you have already added other MFA options to your account, you can select different option to use a third-party authenticator app or a passcode grid.

When to contact us

If you still cannot receive your one-time passcode, you will have to contact us by phone.

More help with multi-factor authentication

Your CRA security code expired

If your CRA security code expires, has been lost, or has not arrived after waiting 10 business days, you may choose instead to use the Interac® document verification service to validate your identity.

If you prefer to receive another CRA security code by mail, you can contact us by phone to get a new one.

Update your information with the CRA

Inform the CRA about changes to your direct deposit or contact information, such as address, phone number or name changes.

Update your information with the CRA

Your account is locked
Your CRA user ID and password was revoked

If your CRA user ID and password have been revoked, you can regain access to your account in either of the following ways:

  • Use a different sign-in option
  • Contact us to register with a new CRA user ID and password

More help with revoked CRA user ID and password

Change the option you sign in with

If you registered with a provincial sign-in partner, you can also create a CRA user ID and password or use an Interac sign-in partner.

If you registered with a CRA user ID and password, you can sign in with your CRA user ID and password and revoke your credential so you can register with a different sign-in option.

If you registered with an Interac sign-in partner, you can sign in to your current Interac sign-in partner and switch to a different bank or credit union.
