Options for registering

There are 3 methods of accessing the different CRA accounts. Choose the option that is best for you.

If you register using a CRA user ID and password or Interac® sign-in service and later decide you want to use a different sign-in option, you will need to call us before you can register again.

CRA user ID and password

You can create a unique CRA user ID and password to sign in to My Account, My Business Account, and Represent a Client.

How to create a strong CRA user ID and password

When you create your CRA user ID and password, we recommend that you:

  • Make them easy for you to remember and hard for others to guess
  • Avoid using personal information such as your name, social insurance number, mailing or email address
  • Always use unique passwords rather than reusing the same password for different accounts or systems
  • Keep this information secure and do not share it with anyone

The only special characters you can use in your user ID and password are:

  • Dot (.)
  • Dash (-)
  • Underscore (_)
  • Apostrophe (‘)

Creating your CRA User ID

Your CRA user ID must contain between 8 and 16 characters with no spaces, and can contain up to seven numbers.

Each CRA user ID must be unique. You will receive a message if the CRA user ID you create has already been used.

Creating your password

Your password must contain between 8 and 64 characters, including:

  • At least one upper-case letter
  • At least one lower-case letter
  • At least one number
  • No space
  • No accented characters

You cannot use more than 4 consecutive, identical characters.

Interac sign-in service

You can use Interac sign-in service to sign in to My Account, My Business Account, and Represent a Client.

Interac sign-in service acts as a secure, online bridge between the Government of Canada and participating banks and credit unions. You may use the same banking information to sign into multiple government services, or you may use different banking information for different government services.

Using your existing online banking information means you don’t have to remember a different user ID and password.

To register with this option, you must:

  • Have an account with one of the participating Sign-in Partners
  • Be registered for online banking
    • If you have lost your bank card, or don't know your bank password and/or user ID

    Contact your financial institution for assistance.

Managing your online banking info

To revoke or change your bank user ID or password or manage your bank security questions, you should contact your financial institution for assistance.

If your bank gives you a new bank card with new numbers, you must contact us by phone to register again using the new card number.

Interac sign-in service and your privacy

When you use a Interac sign-in service, the information you enter and the identity of your financial institution are not shared with the government service you are trying to access. Similarly, information about the government service being accessed is not shared with your financial institution. No personally identifying information or banking details are ever shared.

Because the Interac sign-in service does not provide any identity information to the Government, you must identify yourself by providing some personal information the first time you use this service.

Using a shared device

When you select the Interac sign-in service option and choose your financial institution you will be prompted to enter your banking sign-in information. Some financial institutions will have banking sign-in information pre-populated if you allow that option to be enabled on your device. If this is the case, it is very important to manually enter your own information to ensure your CRA account is linked to your banking sign-in information and not someone who had previously used the device you are signing in with. Once done, follow the prompts to continue with linking to your CRA account.

Provincial partner

You can use a provincial partner to sign in to My Account. You cannot use a provincial partner to sign in to My Business Account or Represent a Client.

The BC Services Card and Alberta.ca Account are each a type of government-issued identification assigned to residents of BC and Alberta.

If you already access My Account using a CRA user ID and password or Interac sign-in service, you may continue using that method.

Signing in/Registering with a provincial partner allows you to:

  • Get immediate access to most account features
  • Sign in/register even if you haven’t filed your taxes in the previous 2 years

With a provincial partner you cannot use:

  • My Business Account or Represent a Client
  • Express Notice of Assessment
  • Auto-fill My Return

For assistance with your BC Services Card, see BC Services Card Login.

For assistance with your Alberta.ca Account, see Alberta.ca Account - Contact.

Privacy and security

The CRA does not share your registration information with any third party. The information you provide is held by the CRA and is protected under the federal Privacy Act.

For more information, see our Privacy notice.
