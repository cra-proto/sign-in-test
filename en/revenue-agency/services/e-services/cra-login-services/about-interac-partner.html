About Interac® sign-in partners

When you choose to access your CRA account with an Interac sign-in partner, you use the same sign-in information you use for your online banking.

What is an Interac sign-in partner

Interac sign-in partners are financial institutions like banks and credit unions that have partnered with Interac to allow their customers to use their online sign-in information to access Government of Canada services.

You may use the same banking information to sign into multiple government services, or you may use different banking information for different government services.

Using your existing online banking information means you don’t have to remember a different user ID and password.

Who can use an Interac sign-in partner

To register with this option, you must:

  • Have a social insurance number (SIN)
  • Have filed your taxes in the last 2 years
  • Have an account with one of the participating sign-in partners
  • Be registered for online banking
    • If you have lost your bank card, or don't know your bank password or user ID

    Contact your financial institution for assistance.

You can use an Interac sign-in partner to sign in to:

  • My Account
  • My Business Account
  • Represent a Client

Managing your online banking credentials

To revoke or change your banking user ID or password or manage your banking security questions, you should contact your financial institution for assistance.

If your bank gives you a new bank card with new numbers, you must contact us by phone to register again using the new card number.

Your privacy and Interac sign-in partners

When you use an Interac sign-in partner, the information you enter and the identity of your financial institution are not shared with the government service you are trying to access. Similarly, information about the government service being accessed is not shared with your financial institution. No personally identifying information or banking details are ever shared.

Because the Interac sign-in partner does not provide any identity information to the Government, you must identify yourself by providing some personal information the first time you use this service.

Using a shared device

When you select the Interac sign-in partner option and choose your financial institution, you will be prompted to enter your banking sign-in information. Some devices allow banking information to pre-populate. Do not use your browser's auto-complete for your online banking information if you register on a shared device. Unless you manually enter your own information, you could link your SIN to someone else's banking information by mistake, and the other person would have access to your CRA account.

Change your sign-in option

You must always sign in to your account with the option you registered with.

If you registered with an Interac sign-in partner and want to change your sign-in option, you can contact us by phone to revoke your credential so you can register with a CRA user ID and password instead.

Switch your Interac sign-in partner

You can switch your Interac sign-in partner as long as you still have access to your account.

  1. Sign in to your account with your Interac sign in partner
  2. Select Switch My Sign-In Partner at the top of the screen
  3. Follow the instructions
