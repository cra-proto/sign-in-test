Set up your business to use Represent a Client

After you have signed in to your Represent a Client (RAC) account, you can register your business and associate employees to it. This will enable you to manage the access of employees to your client's information. Clients can authorize a business as an authorized representative, and only the employees associated to the business can access client information.

On this page

  • Register your business in Represent a Client (RAC)
  • Employee roles and their access
  • Manage employee access

Register your business in Represent a Client (RAC)

You can register your business in RAC using your business number (BN) if all of the following criteria are met:

  • You are identified as the owner, partner, director, or officer of the business in our CRA BN database
  • You have registered your business with the CRA
  • Your social insurance number (SIN) is associated with your business

Sign in to Represent a Client and follow these steps to register your business in RAC:

  • This is your first time signing in to RAC: Option 1

    1. Select Register your business from the welcome screen and follow the instructions provided

      You will also receive a RepID you can give to any client who wants to authorize only you as their representative

  • You have previously signed in to RAC: Option 2

    1. Select Review and update
    2. Select your RepID (if necessary)
    3. In the Manage associations tab, select Register my business with Represent a Client

Once the business is registered in RAC, you will give your BN to clients who want to authorize your business as their representative.

Assigning privileges

You can assign certain privileges to employees that will allow them to perform various tasks. They can be assigned to an employee who doesn't already have them. Where these privileges are assigned to an employee, they will have the ability to perform all of the permissions grouped under that privilege.

View and delete individual clients

The View and delete individual clients privilege groups these 4 permissions:

  • View individual client list
  • Delete Authorizations
  • View individual transactions (Jr. admins have this ability by default)
  • View list of notices issued
View and delete business clients

The View and delete business clients privilege groups these 3 permissions:

  • View business client list
  • Delete Authorizations
  • View business transactions (Jr. admins have this ability by default)
View and delete trust clients

The View and delete trust clients privilege groups these 3 permissions:

  • View trust client list
  • Delete Authorizations
  • View trust transactions (Jr. admins have this ability by default)
Manage access mail

View pre-assessment review and processing review letters that have been electronically delivered.

Manage employee access

Create BN Groups

Any individual client that has authorized a business can be assigned to a BN Group. Employees must then be associated to that group to access the information of those clients.

  1. Sign in to your RAC account
  2. Select Review and update
  3. Select the business name you wish to manage
  4. In the Business information tab, select Create a group
  5. Enter and confirm group information

Associate employees to your business or BN Group

Your employees must register for a RepID through the Register yourself link on the Represent a Client welcome page. When they give you their RepID, you can associate your employees to your business or BN Group to allow them access to your client's information.

  1. Sign in to your RAC account
  2. Select Review and update
  3. Select the business name or BN group you wish to manage
  4. In the Manage employees' access tab, select Associate employees
  5. Select a role for the employee and enter their RepID
    You can also assign additional privileges to individual employees who don't already have them

Assign taxpayers to your BN Group

Only business owners and employees associated to a BN Group will be permitted to access the information of clients assigned to that group.

  1. Sign in to your RAC account
  2. Select Review and update
  3. Select the BN Group you wish to manage
  4. In the Manage clients tab, select Assign taxpayers
  5. Enter taxpayer SINs or business numbers
