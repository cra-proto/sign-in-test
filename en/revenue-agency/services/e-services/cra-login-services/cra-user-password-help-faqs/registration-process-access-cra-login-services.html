Register for a CRA account

I want to register for:
  • My Account for individuals
  • My Business Account
  • Represent a Client

Follow the steps below to register for your accountMy Account for individualsMy Business AccountRepresent a Client and to receive the RepID that you will share with your clients, family or friends so they can authorize you as their representative.

  1. Decide if you need to register

    If you have already registered for another CRA account using a CRA user ID and password or an Interac® sign-in partner, you cannot register again. You must use the same sign-in information to access the following accounts:

    • My Account
    • My Business Account
    • Represent a Client
    Already registered for an account

    Use your existing credentials

    If you have already registered for another CRA account, sign in with your existing account credentials.

    If you registered with a provincial sign-in partner, you must also create a CRA user ID and password or use an Interac sign-in partner to access:

    • My Business Account
    • Represent a Client

    If you have forgotten your CRA user ID or password, go to: Lost or forgotten CRA user ID or password.

    Change the option you sign in with

    If you registered with a provincial sign-in partner, you can also create a CRA user ID and password or use an Interac sign-in partner.

    If you registered with a CRA user ID and password, you can sign in with your CRA user ID and password and revoke your credential so you can register with a different sign-in option.

    If you registered with a Interac sign-in partner, you can sign in to your current Interac sign-in partner and switch to a different bank or credit union.

    Signing in to another account for the first time

    My Business Account

    If you are signing in to My Business Account for the first time, you will be asked for your 9-digit business number.

    Represent a Client

    If you are signing in to Represent a Client for the first time, you will have to register yourself or your business as a representative.

    You will also have to request authorization to access someone else's tax information.

  2. Gather your documents

    When you register with a CRA user ID and password or an Interac sign-in partner, you will need all of the following:

    • Your social insurance number (SIN)
      If you do not have a SIN

      If you don't have a SIN, we will also accept:

      • A temporary tax number (TTN)
      • An individual tax number (ITN)
      • A non-resident representative number (NRRN) if you are registering for Represent a Client
        Applying for a non-resident representative number (NRRN)

        If you are a non-resident representative living in the United States, you can register for Represent a Client with a NRRN to access information and services on behalf of individuals and businesses.

        1. Submit Form RC391, Application for a Canada Revenue Agency Non-Resident Representative Number (NRRN), and provide supporting documentation to prove your identity
        2. Wait until you receive a letter in the mail with your NRRN
        3. Register for Represent a Client with your NRRN instead of a SIN
    • Your date of birth
    • The amounts you reported on your most recent tax return
      Which tax return you can use

      The tax return you use must meet both of the following conditions:

      • It was filed and assessed
      • It is from either the current or previous tax year

      Use only the amounts that you reported on your return, even if your return was reassessed and the amounts have changed.

    • If you are registering for a My Business Account you also need your 9-digit business number
      • You cannot use a 10-digit Québec Enterprise Number (NEQ)

    For faster access to all features, you will also need a mobile device with a working camera and one of the following:

    • Your Canadian passport
    • Your Canadian driver's license
    • Your provincial or territorial photo ID card

    When you register with a provincial sign-in partner, you only need your social insurance number (SIN).

    Have your documents ready before you begin

    During registration, your session will expire after 18 minutes of inactivity. Gather your documents before you begin to avoid your session timing out.

  3. Choose a sign-in option to register for your account

    The option you register with will be the same option you use each time you sign in. Make a selection below to begin creating your account.

    Find the best option for your situation

    Learn more about the sign-in options for your account:

    CRA user ID and password
    This option lets you create a username and password that you only use with your CRA accounts.
    Interac sign-in partner
    This option lets you use the same sign-in information you use for online banking. You must already have an online account with one of our sign-in partner banks or credit unions.
    Provincial sign-in partner
    If you already have a BC Services Card or an Alberta.ca Account, you can use them to immediately access your My Account.
    The first time you sign in, you will need to enter your SIN to validate your identity. You will not need to use Interac document verification service or a CRA security code to validate your identity.
    You can register with your provincial sign-in partner even if you have not filed your taxes in the last 2 years, as long as you have filed your taxes at least once in the past.
    You cannot use your provincial sign-in partner to access My Business Account, Represent a Client, and certain services like Express Notice of Assessment or Auto-fill My Return. If you would like to access those accounts or services, you can still create a CRA user ID and password or use an Interac sign-in partner.
    GC Key
    You cannot use GC Key to sign in to the CRA accounts.

  4. Validate your identity

    Choose an option to validate your identity:

    • Submit a photo of yourself and your ID document Option 1 of 2

      You can use the Interac document verification service to validate your identity immediately.

      Use a mobile device to take a real-time picture of yourself and an accepted identification document.

      You must be 16 years of age or older to use this service.

    • Wait for your CRA security code Option 2 of 2

      If you can't use the document verification service, a CRA security code will be sent to you by mail within 5 to 10 business days.

      We will send your CRA security code to the address we have on file. Make sure your address is up to date.

      You will need to sign in to your account and enter your CRA security code before it expires.

    For My Account only: Get limited access before validating your identity

    You do not need to validate your identity to access the following features:

    • View status of your tax return
    • View RRSP deduction limit and TFSA contribution room
    • View notice of assessment or reassessment
    • Request a remittance voucher
    • Pay by pre-authorized debit (create new agreement)
    • Change notification preferences

    After you validate your identity, you will have access to additional features such as applying for benefits and updating your personal information.

  5. Enroll in multi-factor authentication (MFA)

    Most users will be required to enter a one-time passcode in addition to their password.

    Choose an option to get your one-time passcode:

    • By phone Option 1 of 2

      You will need to provide at least one cell or landline number so we can send you a one-time passcode by phone. You must enter this number to sign in to your account.

    • With a passcode grid Option 2 of 3

      The system will generate a unique passcode grid that you will need to save or print. You will use this grid every time you sign in to your account.

      Your passcode grid will expire after 18 months. Make sure you sign in and generate a new one before the expiry date.

    • With an authenticator app Option 3 of 3

      You can use a third-party authenticator app to generate your time-based one-time passcode.

      When prompted, use the app on your mobile device to either scan a QR code or manually enter the setup key. The app can then be used to generate your one-time passcode.

    Avoid getting locked out of your account

    We recommend that you enroll in more than one option, although only one option is required. Enrolling in multiple MFA options will help ensure that you can still access your account if you change your phone number, misplace your passcode grid, or delete the authenticator app.

    If your account becomes locked due to entering the wrong one-time passcode too many times, you will be temporarily locked out for 30 minutes. After that period, you can try again. If this happens 3 times, you will be permanently locked out, and will need to contact us by phone.

    More help with multi-factor authentication

  6. Complete your identity validation

    Complete the validation option that you chose in step 4:

    • Interac document verification service Option 1 of 2

      Follow the instructions to complete this process on your mobile device.

      Avoid losing your progress

      During registration, your session will expire after 18 minutes of inactivity. Complete your document verification within that time to avoid your session timing out.

    • CRA security code Option 2 of 2

      After you receive your CRA security code in the mail, sign in to your accountsign in to My Accountsign in to My Business Accountsign in to Represent a Client and enter your code before it expires.

      Get a new CRA security code

      If your code expires, gets lost, or has not arrived after 10 business days, you can contact us by phone to get a new one.

  7. For Represent a Client only:
    Get authorized to access someone else's account

    You must request authorization from your client, family member or friend to access their account.

    Request authorization for your business

    If you would like to access someone else's tax information through your business, you must register your business in Represent a Client and then request authorization with your business number (BN).

    Learn more: Set up your business in Represent a Client

Help centre

Common issues for registration

Have not filed your taxes

If you have not filed your taxes for the current or previous tax year, you will not be able to register for My Account using a CRA user ID and password or an Interac sign-in partner. You also won't be able to register for My Business Account or Represent a Client.

For information on how to file, go to Get ready to do your taxes.

If you live in British Columbia or Alberta, you may be able to register for My Account using a provincial sign-in partner.

Have not received your CRA security code

If your CRA security code expires, has been lost, or has not arrived after waiting 10 business days, you may instead choose to use the Interac document verification service to validate your identity.

If you prefer to receive another CRA security code by mail, you can contact us by phone to get a new one.

Interac sign-in partners

Accepted identification documents

We accept the following original government-issued documents:

  • Your Canadian passport
  • Your Canadian driver's license
  • Your provincial or territorial photo ID card

We do not accept:

  • Your provincial health card
  • Documents that you have reported as lost or stolen
  • Expired or damaged documents
  • Photocopies

One-time passcode by phone

When you sign in, you can choose to receive your one-time passcode by:

  • Text message
  • Automated phone call from a toll-free number

One-time passcode by passcode grid

The passcode grid is a table made up of numbered rows and lettered columns, similar to a Bingo card.

We will ask for a specific combination of rows and columns to create your one-time passcode.
